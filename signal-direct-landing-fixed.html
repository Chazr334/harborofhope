Our Mission

Harbor of Hope is a volunteer-led network built for uncertain times. Inspired by the quiet strength of the Underground Railroad, we work to connect people with resources—shelter, support, information, and more—without putting them in the spotlight. Some of us stand in the open. Some of us move in the dark. All of us are here to make sure no one slips through the cracks. We don't make promises we can't keep. But we show up, and we help however we can.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do you use Signal? ▼

Signal provides true end-to-end encryption, meaning only the participants in a conversation can read the messages. Signal also has features like disappearing messages, screen security, and minimal metadata collection. It's widely regarded as the most secure mainstream messaging platform.

What if I can't join the Signal group? ▼

Some networks or browsers might block Signal links. If you're experiencing difficulties, try using a different network, device, or browser. As a last resort, email us and we'll provide alternate instructions.

How are donations used? ▼

Funds go directly to emergency housing, transportation, legal fee assistance, and encrypted communication tools. All spending is transparent to verified network members.

Safety guidelines ▼

Always use encrypted channels, verify member identity through multiple steps, never share locations publicly, and keep detailed records off network devices.

Can I use an alias? ▼

Yes, we encourage the use of aliases for added privacy. You never need to share your legal name with us.

What happens after I contact you? ▼

Our screening group administrators will have a brief conversation with you to understand your needs or how you'd like to contribute. This helps us ensure network security and find the best way to include you. After screening, approved members will be added to our main Signal group.
